    UNDERSTAND THE BIGGER PICTURE

    What does socioeconomic mobility mean?

    Socioeconomic mobility is the ability of a person or family to increase stability, options, decision-making power, and wellbeing over time or across generations.

    Data should guide questions—not label people

    Community data describe patterns across groups and places. They do not determine one person’s potential, character, or future.
